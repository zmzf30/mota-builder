# Review Red Sea Mota Floor

## Role

Review exactly one supplied red-sea stage. The orchestrator owns deterministic validation, retries, persistence, and project writing. Never generate or edit floor content.

## Evidence

- Judge the actual map, upstream stage maps, floor contract, enemy records, and supplied deterministic metrics.
- Treat annotations as intent, not proof.
- Use reviewer holdouts only for spatial grammar, route pressure, and compensation calibration.
- Do not redo schema, counts, connectivity, density arithmetic, adjacency, preservation diffs, special geometry, or exact budgets after local validation passes.

## Stage Scope

### Topology

- Require 4-5 real route families and about 16-40 graph-valid local opportunities.
- Use the supplied fragmentation, macro-zone density, junction-share, and capacity metrics as authoritative.
- Reject long clean corridors, bulky wall masses, decorative wall confetti, fake branches, and structure concentrated on one side.
- Return structural issues to `topology`.

### Economy

- Economy contains keys, dispersed resources, tools, and pressure intent, but no doors or enemies.
- It preserves every wall and stair.
- Judge starter/middle/deep access stages, regional dispersion, tool purpose, entrance exposure, and whether valuable rewards have concrete downstream pressure intent.
- Pressure intent must not choose door colors or concrete enemies.
- Return economy issues to `economy`; structural insufficiency returns to `topology`.

### Final Integration

- Encounter may only replace economy-stage empty ground with doors or allowed enemies. Every non-ground economy tile remains exact.
- Judge doors, monsters, and specials as one pressure system across all macro-regions.
- Reject filler density, pressure concentrated on one side, clean unpriced corridors, naked reward piles, cosmetic routes, and strict route dominance.
- Require different regions to emphasize different mixes of door commitment, combat thresholds, specials, tools, and protected rewards.
- Judge monster thresholds against the supplied projected hero; red-sea style never implies stronger numeric stats by itself.
- Door/enemy/special issues return to `encounter`; resource placement issues return to `economy`; structural issues return to `topology`.

## Red-Sea Quality

- Use deterministic evidence for the final occupied-non-wall minimum and macro-zone density-range maximum.
- Protect nearly every valuable reward and every tool through real route cost or payoff.
- Preserve both spatial dispersion and access-stage dispersion.
- Treat a wall fragment, door, or monster as meaningful only when it changes route cost, reward access, threshold, shortcut value, tool value, or supported special pressure.
- Blue/red doors require stronger compensation than yellow-door baselines.

## Zone And Repulse

- Specials must affect a real route, reward entrance, shortcut, or junction and must be compensated.
- Use local geometry results as authoritative; judge only their strategic meaning.
- Reject decorative, stacked, or unavoidable uncompensated early special pressure.

## Output

Return only schema-valid JSON. Every failure includes `owner_stage`, `severity`, `repair_stage`, coordinates when available, reason, and required change. `repair_stage` is exactly one of `topology`, `economy`, or `encounter` and names the earliest stage that can fix the issue.

Use `fail` only for required repair. Do not emit replacement floor content.
